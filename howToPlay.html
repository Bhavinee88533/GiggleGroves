Steps TO Play:

SIMON SAYS GAME

  • Start the Game: Press the "Start Game" button to begin.
  • Watch : The game will show you a sequence by lighting up the buttons.
  • Repeat the Sequence: Click the buttons in the same order as the sequence you just saw.
  • Increase the Challenge: With each correct sequence you repeat, the game will add one more step to the sequence. See how long you can keep up!
  • Game Over: If you click the wrong button, the game ends. Try again to beat your previous score!

STONE PAPER SCISSORS

  • Preparation:Two players stand facing each other.
  • Choose and Declare:Players count to three aloud together, each saying "One, two, three" in unison.On the count of three, each player simultaneously forms one of three shapes with their hand:
    Stone (Rock): Form a fist.
    Paper: Hold out an open hand with fingers extended.
    Scissors: Extend the index and middle fingers, forming a V.
  • Determine the Winner:Compare the shapes shown by each player:
    Stone crushes Scissors: Stone wins.
    Scissors cuts Paper: Scissors win.
    Paper covers Stone: Paper wins.
    If both players choose the same shape, the game is a tie and must be replayed.

TIC TAC TOE

  • Preparation:
    Draw a 3x3 grid on a piece of paper or use a game board with a 3x3.
    The grid consists of nine squares, arranged in three rows and three columns.
  • Choose Symbols:
    One player chooses the symbol 'X' and the other player chooses 'O'.
    Traditionally, the player using 'X' goes first, but this can be decided by mutual agreement or a coin toss.
  • Taking Turns:
    Players take turns placing their symbol (either 'X' or 'O') in one of the empty squares on the grid.
    The goal is to be the first player to get three of their symbols in a row, either horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
  • Winning the Game:
    The first player to align three of their symbols in a row (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally) wins the game.
    If all nine squares are filled and neither player has three in a row, the game is a tie (also called a "cat's game" or "draw").
  • Repeat:
    Players can reset the grid and play multiple rounds, taking turns starting each game.
